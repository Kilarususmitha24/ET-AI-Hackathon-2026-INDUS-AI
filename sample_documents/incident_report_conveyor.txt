Incident Report - Conveyor Belt Failure
Document ID: INC-CV102-2024
Category: Incident

INCIDENT SUMMARY:
Date: 2024-04-08 14:30
Location: Packaging Line 2
Equipment: Conveyor System CV-102
Severity: MEDIUM
Reported by: Shift Supervisor A. Kumar

DESCRIPTION:
During peak production shift, Conveyor CV-102 experienced sudden belt slippage
causing material spillage and production stoppage. Emergency stop was activated.
No injuries reported. Production downtime: 3.5 hours.

SYMPTOMS OBSERVED:
- Belt tracking deviation to drive side
- Unusual squealing noise 2 hours prior to failure
- Belt tension visibly reduced
- Spillage at transfer point TP-102

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary: Belt tension loss due to worn tensioner assembly
Contributing factors:
1. Tensioner not inspected during last PM (overdue by 2 weeks)
2. Increased load from production ramp-up
3. Roller bearing wear on idler station IS-102C

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
1. Replaced belt tensioner assembly
2. Replaced worn idler roller bearings (IS-102A, IS-102C)
3. Adjusted belt tracking and tension to specification
4. Updated PM checklist to include tensioner inspection

PREVENTIVE MEASURES:
- Add belt tension measurement to weekly inspection
- Install belt slip detection sensor
- Reduce maximum load during ramp-up periods

REGULATORY IMPACT:
- OSHA 1910.147: LOTO verified during repair - COMPLIANT
- ISO 45001: Incident investigation completed within 48 hours - COMPLIANT
- No EPA reportable quantities involved

RELATED EQUIPMENT:
- Motor M-102 (5 HP)
- Drive pulley DP-102
- Belt: 48" wide, 3-ply rubber

MAINTENANCE RECOMMENDATION:
Inspect all conveyor systems for similar tensioner wear.
Priority: MEDIUM
Estimated inspection time: 1.5 hours per conveyor
