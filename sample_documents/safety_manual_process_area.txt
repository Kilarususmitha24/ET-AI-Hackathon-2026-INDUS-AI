Safety Manual - Process Area Operations
Document ID: SAF-PA-2024
Category: Safety

SECTION 1: GENERAL SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
All personnel entering Process Area must complete safety orientation training annually.
Required PPE: Hard hat, safety glasses, steel-toe boots, flame-resistant clothing.

SECTION 2: LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURES
Per OSHA 1910.147, all energy sources must be isolated before maintenance:
- Electrical: Lock MCC breakers, verify with voltage tester
- Mechanical: Block moving parts, release stored energy
- Hydraulic: Depressurize systems, lock valves
- Pneumatic: Bleed air lines, lock supply valves
- Thermal: Allow cool-down period, verify temperature below 50°C

SECTION 3: CONFINED SPACE ENTRY
Per OSHA 1910.146:
- Obtain confined space entry permit
- Atmospheric testing: O2 (19.5-23.5%), LEL (<10%), H2S (<10 ppm)
- Station attendant at entry point
- Rescue equipment readily available

SECTION 4: FIRE SAFETY (NFPA 70E)
Electrical safety requirements:
- Arc flash hazard analysis required for panels >240V
- Arc-rated PPE based on incident energy calculation
- Minimum approach distances per voltage class
- Ground fault protection on all portable tools

SECTION 5: ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE (EPA 40 CFR)
- Spill prevention: SPCC plan maintained and updated annually
- Waste management: Hazardous waste manifests required
- Emissions monitoring: Continuous monitoring for boiler stack
- Reportable quantities: Immediate notification for spills >threshold

SECTION 6: EMERGENCY RESPONSE
Fire: Activate alarm, evacuate to Assembly Point B
Chemical spill: Contain, notify EHS, initiate spill response team
Medical emergency: Call 911, notify plant nurse station

SECTION 7: PROCESS PIPING (ASME B31.3)
- All piping modifications require engineering review
- Pressure testing per code requirements before return to service
- Flange bolt torque per manufacturer specifications

REGULATORY REFERENCES:
OSHA 1910.147, OSHA 1910.146, NFPA 70E, EPA 40 CFR, ASME B31.3, ISO 45001
